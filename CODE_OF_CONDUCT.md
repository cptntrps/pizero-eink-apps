# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Pi Zero 2W Application Suite community is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors and users, regardless of background or experience level.

We are dedicated to:
- Creating a **safe and respectful** community
- Fostering **collaboration** and **innovation**
- Treating all members with **dignity and respect**
- Valuing **diverse perspectives** and **experiences**

## Our Values

### Respect
- Listen to others with an open mind
- Value different viewpoints
- Disagree constructively
- Treat others as you wish to be treated

### Inclusion
- Welcome contributors of all backgrounds
- Support newcomers and beginners
- Create accessible communication
- Ensure everyone has a voice

### Integrity
- Communicate honestly
- Give proper credit
- Take responsibility for your actions
- Follow through on commitments

### Excellence
- Help each other learn and grow
- Celebrate successes
- Learn from failures
- Continuously improve

## Expected Behavior

### Be Respectful
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be patient and understanding
- Acknowledge different expertise levels
- Respect confidentiality

### Be Collaborative
- Work together toward common goals
- Share knowledge generously
- Give constructive feedback
- Support others' success

### Be Professional
- Keep discussions focused on the topic
- Avoid off-topic arguments
- Respect time and effort
- Maintain professional conduct

### Be Safe
- Don't harass or discriminate
- Don't share private information
- Don't use offensive language
- Don't engage in violence or threats

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

### Harassment
- Verbal attacks or insults
- Deliberate intimidation
- Threats of violence
- Unwelcome sexual advances
- Targeted exclusion

### Discrimination
- Based on race, color, ethnicity
- Based on gender or gender identity
- Based on religion or beliefs
- Based on disability
- Based on age or experience level
- Based on sexual orientation
- Any other protected characteristic

### Abuse
- Name-calling or mocking
- Doxing (sharing private information)
- Spam or excessive self-promotion
- Trolling or intentional disruption
- Spreading misinformation

### Other Violations
- Plagiarism or copyright infringement
- Violation of privacy
- Violation of intellectual property
- Illegal activity

## Reporting Issues

### If You Witness Inappropriate Behavior

1. **Address it directly** (if safe):
   - "That language is not welcome here"
   - "That comment was disrespectful"
   - Assume good intent initially

2. **Document the behavior**:
   - Save screenshots or links
   - Note date, time, and participants
   - Preserve evidence

3. **Report it**:
   - Email: [maintainer-email-here]
   - Subject: "Code of Conduct Violation"
   - Include details of the incident

### Reporting Process

1. **Initial Report**
   - Contact maintainers with incident details
   - All reports are taken seriously
   - Confidentiality is maintained

2. **Investigation**
   - Gathering facts from all parties
   - Treating everyone fairly
   - Maintaining transparency
   - Allowing opportunity for response

3. **Resolution**
   - Appropriate action taken
   - Prevention of future violations
   - Support for affected parties
   - Documentation for patterns

4. **Follow-up**
   - Check that behavior has changed
   - Support for reporting party
   - Appeal process available

## Consequences

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:

### Minor Violations
- Verbal or written warning
- Request for behavior change
- Temporary suspension
- Required education/training

### Serious Violations
- Immediate removal from community
- Ban from future participation
- Loss of commit privileges
- Public statement (if appropriate)

### Repeated Violations
- Permanent ban
- Report to authorities (if necessary)
- Action to prevent harm

## Enforcement

The community leadership will:
- Clarify standards of acceptable behavior
- Investigate violations promptly
- Enforce consistently and fairly
- Respect privacy during investigation
- Provide fair appeal process

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All community spaces (GitHub, forums, chat, events)
- Official project representation
- Community-organized events
- Any space where project business is conducted

When representing the project, apply these standards in all interactions.

## Specific Guidelines

### Online Spaces

#### GitHub Issues and Discussions
- Stay on topic
- Respect license and copyright
- No spam or self-promotion
- Report abusive comments

#### Pull Request Reviews
- Give constructive feedback
- Focus on code, not person
- Appreciate effort and time
- Be encouraging to new contributors

#### Chat and Communications
- Use professional language
- No harassment or exclusion
- Respect different time zones
- Keep discussions on-topic

### In-Person Events

#### Meetups and Conferences
- Create safe spaces
- Respect personal boundaries
- Report incidents to organizers
- Support fellow attendees

#### Representation
- Represent the project professionally
- Follow event Code of Conduct
- Be inclusive and welcoming
- Model good behavior

## Special Considerations

### Power Dynamics
- Maintainers have responsibility to set example
- Avoid abuse of power or influence
- Be extra cautious about relationships
- Recuse from decisions involving close associates

### Newcomers
- Welcome new contributors warmly
- Provide guidance and mentorship
- Explain project norms and expectations
- Be patient with learning curve

### Minorities
- Take extra care in inclusion
- Actively solicit diverse perspectives
- Address microaggressions promptly
- Create safe spaces for all

## FAQ

### Q: What if I make a mistake?
A: We believe in learning. If you violate the Code of Conduct:
- Acknowledge the mistake
- Apologize sincerely
- Change your behavior
- Most first violations won't result in permanent action

### Q: Is this really enforced?
A: Yes. The Code of Conduct is taken seriously and violations are investigated and acted upon.

### Q: What if I disagree with the decision?
A: You can appeal to the maintainers with additional information or context. Fair process is guaranteed.

### Q: Can I remain anonymous when reporting?
A: Yes. Anonymity is respected, but some details may be necessary for investigation.

### Q: What if the accused is a maintainer?
A: Violations by maintainers are treated seriously. They may recuse themselves, and external review may be sought.
